Installation of a 2-Ton Goodman Heat Pump Split System

This project involved the complete installation of a 2-ton Goodman heat pump split system, designed to deliver efficient heating and cooling for a residential space. The installation was performed with attention to detail, safety, and adherence to best HVAC practices to ensure long-term reliability and energy efficiency.

1. Ductwork Preparation

The installation began with the setup of 6 in. x 25 ft. Insulated Flexible Duct R6 Silver Jacket.This duct features a metalized polyester vapor barrier reinforced with fiberglass scrim, offering excellent insulation, strength, and tear resistance. The flexible ducts were carefully routed to provide efficient air distribution throughout the home.

2. Supply Trunk Installation

The main supply trunk was installed as the central air distribution pathway. Openings were measured and cut precisely on the trunk to connect the flexible ducts. Nylon straps were used to secure the flexible ducts to the joists, while metal straps were used to firmly support and stabilize the main trunk, reducing vibration and maintaining alignment.

3. Return Vent Construction

A 20" x 20" return vent was constructed and installed to ensure balanced airflow and efficient system performance. Proper sealing and positioning helped maintain optimal air circulation back to the air handler.

4. Air Handler Setup

A secure platform was built on the ceiling to hold the drip pan and air handler. Once the platform was in place, the air handler was positioned and leveled. The supply and return trunks were then connected to the air handler, and all joints were sealed and insulated to prevent condensation and thermal loss.

5. Refrigerant Line and Drain System

The refrigerant line set was connected and brazed to the air handler with precision to ensure airtight connections. A condensate drain hose and float switch were installed to manage condensation safely and protect against potential overflow. All components were tested for proper fit and drainage flow.

6. Outdoor Unit Installation

The outdoor condenser unit was set on a level pad and aligned with the indoor components. The refrigerant lines were brazed to the outdoor service valves, and all connections were checked for quality and strength. A thermostat wire was routed from the air handler to the control area, ensuring proper communication between the indoor and outdoor units. The control wiring was neatly secured and connected according to manufacturer specifications.
Note: Electrical connections were completed by a licensed electrician after the mechanical installation.

7. System Testing and Commissioning

After completing mechanical connections, a pressure test with nitrogen was performed, followed by a vacuum test down to 300 microns. Once confirmed leak-free, the refrigerant was released into the system, completing the installation.
